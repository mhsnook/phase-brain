Three-layer memory network

Felt-category binding (innermost, most resilient) → Referential binding (middle) → Temporal-sequence binding (outermost, most fragile). When a node loses its own-layer binding for long enough, it can misbind to a node in a different layer — briefly taking on that layer's behavior — before eventually snapping back or re-stabilizing.
